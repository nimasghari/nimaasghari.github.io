Why Email is a Big Topic for any business, even tiny ones

Email is integrated into all our daily lives, but the perspective changes entirely when you’re thinking in terms of business.

Emails are still a super effective and practical way to keep in touch with potential customers and build report with current ones. Which offers a reason why you’re being asked for an email address everywhere you turn on the internet. Free services like, resume generators will ask for your email address to email give you the file you just spent time working on. Still, you wonder just how valuable your email address is to these companies.

It’s important to ask in the name of your business, where you can implement email marketing, and you really should wherever possible. Whether it’s industry related tips, new products and offers, or a modest email that asks customers if they received their order on time, and if everything is okay – such activity can build a strong relationship between you and your subscribers.

Large contact lists that have the consent of their audience are proof that you have provided a large crowd with quality content. To create an incentive for people to subscribe is not an easy thing to do, you have to provide value. I’ve told Realtors and Mortgage Brokers for example, to ask for the email address of every person they engage with. And every person you engage with via email, ask them if they’re okay with receiving content from you regularly. If your content is good and informative to the person, they will be more than glad to support your list.

Such practice sets people apart in many professions, it’s long term thinking that can pay off tremendously and make you look good as a business. It’s proof of dedication, passion, and much more. That’s how a list by itself is a testament to good and honest business.

Subscriber Lists are Free Advertisement. When you’re connected with every past and potential customer with email, you’re basically able to advertise to any willing number of people for free. Even if your content has nothing to do with closing a sale, your brand name appearing in people’s inboxes is solid and free exposure. With a large and clean list, you can even offer affiliate advertising by promoting someone else’s product.

Subscribers Stay Subscribed – just don’t offend them. It’s just not worth the time to click on an email and unsubscribe from it, we may even like the stimulation of just getting another email – like a text message, knowing that we exist to someone out there must feel good. Most of the time I’m even afraid to click the unsubscribe button if I don’t recognize a company – I don’t trust unknown links. So the idea is, when you constantly dish out content that’s friendly, and acknowledge your audience’s existence without offending them, you’ll hold on to most subscribers for many years. A contact list will only grow if you’re consistent, and eventually it can snowball into something incredibly valuable. Stay on topic with the content people subscribed for, don’t throw sales pitches or all caps subject lines, and you’ll mostly do fine.

Stay on track and be consistent, your audience will notice. Email marketing can take time to generate sales but the results will be solid. Even if most people react to your emails and read them, sales will take time. In many cases people will finally give you a try after months of seeing you send out consistently good content. It acknowledges to potential a customer that you’re a reliable brand that is there to stay.

It’s not required but definitely great if you can match the tone of your emails with the rest of your business and content. Keeping the same overall design in your emails can start to give some of your readers nostalgia over time. By maintaining the same image over long periods, you’ll be a familiar face.

Most marketing emails include a call to action like, “Click here to login to your account and view your new message” or “Start Shopping” after you’ve been enticed by some trendy material. That link will take you to a landing page which should flow well based on the content before.

Whether it’s a landing page for people to buy or sign up for a new product, or for people to continue reading, in email marketing you will analyze all behavior to increase conversion rates.

Subject lines are the best opportunity you have to get someone to open an email. The preview text is what most email clients show next to the subject – which is customizable. The most successful email subjects are ones that contain relevant information, are short, and friendly. For example, “Hey Kathy, I’ve got some awesome new properties in your area”. Inserting a person’s name in a subject as if you would write to a friend is easy, and it adds the most impact to a subject line. Email marketers can analyze who opens an email for how long, and from where.

Scheduling posts is good practice, and with a few attempts you can zone in on the hours of the day that your audience will be most. A good campaign can have from 3 months to even 3 years of scheduled posts. For example if you have a niche food blog, you can have 80 recipes scheduled to be sent once a week for over a year. So even if you slacked off or didn’t have content to share for some time, you’ll be happy to know that automated emails are going out.

A/B testing applies to emails just as much as anywhere else.

A/B testing is simple, let’s say you’re sending out a promotional email to 10,000 viewers. You can send out two variations – A and B. So 5,000 people will get Email A and the rest will get Email B – to help a marketer analyze the impact of certain content. When in doubt, A/B testing can tell you what works and what doesn’t.

Conditional Emails are another potentially intricate addition to run a stellar campaign or newsletter. Sending emails based on conditions entails things like, “To the subscribers that opened Monday’s email, send them newsletter X on Wednesday.” Or, “If subscriber added shopping items to cart but did not finish a purchase, send them an email three days later reminding them that they have items in their cart.” As you can probably tell right away, the possibilities become endless.

In Conclusion, integrating emails wherever possible in your business will likely bring value. And with the technicalities involved, long term structuring, and complicated nature of engaging with a target audience, it’s important to consult with or think of different ways that email can play a role in your model.
